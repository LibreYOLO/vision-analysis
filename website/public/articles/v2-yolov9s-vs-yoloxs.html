YOLOv9-S vs YOLOX-S: The Fairest Comparison on NVIDIA A100

COCO val2017 · 5,000 images · PyTorch FP32 · March 2026 · Benchmarked with LibreYOLO v0.1.2

The "S" variants from each family — this is the closest head-to-head possible. YOLOv9-S achieves 38.3 mAP@50-95 at 28.0 FPS with 7.23M parameters. YOLOX-S achieves 34.6 mAP@50-95 at 44.0 FPS with 8.97M parameters. YOLOv9-S is 10.7% more accurate with 19% fewer parameters, but YOLOX-S is 57% faster. Three years of progress (ECCV 2024 vs arXiv 2021) bought more accuracy per parameter at the cost of GPU throughput. Both benchmarked with LibreYOLO v0.1.2, retrained weights, COCO train2017.

The FLOP-matched comparison: Both models use ~13.5 GFLOPs — but YOLOv9-S extracts 3.7 more mAP points from the same compute budget. This is the clearest evidence that PGI and GELAN are more information-efficient architectures than CSPDarknet + PAFPN.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better, YOLOv9-S or YOLOX-S?
YOLOv9-S (ECCV 2024) is more accurate: 38.3 vs 34.6 mAP with fewer parameters (7.23M vs 8.97M). YOLOX-S (Megvii, 2021) is 57% faster: 44 vs 28 FPS. Choose YOLOv9-S for accuracy, YOLOX-S for throughput. Both available in LibreYOLO.
Why is YOLOX-S faster at similar FLOPs?
Both use ~13.5 GFLOPs, but YOLOX's CSPDarknet convolutions are standard operations highly optimized on GPUs. YOLOv9's GELAN uses more complex gradient pathways that don't parallelize as efficiently on A100 CUDA cores.
