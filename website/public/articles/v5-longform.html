YOLOv9-T vs YOLOX-S: Complete Benchmark Analysis

NVIDIA A100 · COCO val2017 · PyTorch FP32 · March 22, 2026

TL;DR

YOLOX-S is the better all-round model: 34.6 mAP at 44 FPS vs 30.2 mAP at 23 FPS. But YOLOv9-T is the efficiency king — it delivers 3.8x more mAP per million parameters, making it the better choice for edge and resource-constrained deployment. Both are available in LibreYOLO.

Background

YOLOv9 and YOLOX represent two different eras and philosophies in YOLO design. YOLOX, released by Megvii in 2021, introduced an anchor-free approach with a decoupled head. YOLOv9, from 2024, pushes the boundary of parameter efficiency with Programmable Gradient Information (PGI) and the Generalized Efficient Layer Aggregation Network (GELAN).

We're comparing the smallest variants of each family: YOLOv9-T (tiny, 2.04M params) vs YOLOX-S (small, 8.97M params). While not an apples-to-apples size comparison, these are the models practitioners actually consider against each other — they're both at the "small model" end of their respective families.

YOLOX-S leads by 4.4 mAP@50-95 points, a meaningful gap. The delta widens to 8.8 points at the looser mAP@50 threshold, suggesting YOLOX-S is particularly better at rough localization.

Precision vs Recall tradeoff: YOLOv9-T is the more "conservative" detector — it fires less often but is right more often (61.5% precision). YOLOX-S is more aggressive, finding 57% of all objects but with a higher false positive rate. For applications like surveillance where missing an object is worse than a false alarm, YOLOX-S's high recall is the better tradeoff.

Speed & Latency

Latency p9957.8 ms29.2 ms

This is perhaps the most surprising result. Despite having 4.4x more parameters, YOLOX-S runs 1.9x faster on the A100. This comes down to architecture: YOLOX uses straightforward convolutions that GPUs are highly optimized for. YOLOv9's more complex gradient information pathways introduce overhead that parameter count alone doesn't predict.

Tail latency matters. YOLOX-S has remarkably tight latency: p99 is only 28% above p50 (29.2 vs 22.8 ms). YOLOv9-T shows more variance: p99 is 37% above p50 (57.8 vs 42.3 ms). For real-time applications with strict SLAs, YOLOX-S is more predictable.

YOLOv9-T's efficiency numbers are exceptional. With 3.8x more mAP per million parameters and 3.0x more mAP per GFLOP, it demonstrates the effectiveness of the PGI approach at extracting maximum performance from minimal compute.

When to Use Each

Edge / Embedded → YOLOv9-T

Deploying to Raspberry Pi, Jetson Nano, or mobile? YOLOv9-T's 2M parameter footprint means it fits in tight memory budgets and benefits most from quantization. Its high mAP-per-FLOP means you get the most accuracy out of limited compute. Try it with LibreYOLO's ONNX or NCNN export.

Cloud / Server → YOLOX-S

On A100s, V100s, or similar GPUs where memory and compute aren't constrained, YOLOX-S gives you more accuracy at higher throughput. Its tight latency distribution makes it ideal for real-time serving. Its higher recall means fewer missed detections in production.
